Glad

Connecting people
by common interests,
monetizing hobbies
and skills.

My role

Product Designer

The context

Did you know that people tend to isolate themselves more and more from the world
and that loneliness is already seen as a social problem?
Thinking about this scenario, I and some entrepreneurial friends thought of a solution
that connects people, offering company and experience according to their affinity.

The product

Just as the Uber driver offers a ride and an owner on Airbnb offers accommodation,
anyone at Glad can offer some kind of experience and charge a fee for it.
It can be a guitar lesson, a tour guide through the city, a chess match in the square or
simply a company to sing in karaoke.
On the one hand, someone makes money from their skills and hobbies.
On the other hand, someone has a new experience, meets a new person and, who
knows, builds a new friendship.

The first versions of Glad can now be downloaded in Brazilian app stores and are
gradually being migrated to the current design of this page.

User interviews

Why it was essential to pivot even
before thinking about solution.

I talked to 30 people (US and Brazil) and there is resistance when it
comes to renting friends.
Maybe because renting a friend is assuming that you are lonely and have
no friends, even if that is not true.
But there is no problem in paying someone to perform some activity.

We had already decided that the core of communication would be the
rental of friends. But when I brought these findings to the Glad team,
we decided to remove the word “friends”.

We would no longer be an app for renting friends, but an app to connect
people with common affinities and interests.

A clear
interface for a
clear goal.

The main screen of the product is a list of
people with different skills who registered
offering some type of activity or subject
of interest.

Profile and
open chat.

In the user's profile there is a mini bio, the
amount charged per hour, the reviews of
previous bookings and an open chat to set
up the activitie, the day and the time.

Safety and
visibility

Safety first!
The entire booking process is controlled
by the users: The booking request,
cancellation, start, and even abort the
activity and finish the booking for any
reason whatsoever.

Lessons and the
ouctomes so far

We are still using the old design in the store to learn as much as possible from the
mistakes and we are gradually migrating to the "final" version that I presented here.

Among the main things we learned, the main thing was not having tested it
extensively before launching and that would save us a lot of time to develop and redo
solutions. We launched two apps - for regular users and for Pro (like Uber and Uber
driver) - and this is causing a lot of confusion among users and generating a huge
communication effort on our part.
