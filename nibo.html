One

Design System

and thousands in savings to the company.

My role

Product Designer (and salesman)

The context

Do you know that phrase “Move Fast and Break Things”?
In almost every startup, nobody fixes what was broken.
At Nibo, I was tired of spending more time finding the pieces and little time thinking
about solutions.

The product

This is not a technical article about Design System, nor a great case full of post its on
how I saved the world.
It's about how I convinced my stakeholders to put time and energy to organize the
house, fix broken things, improve processes in the company, the day-to-day life of the
team and saving money.
All at once.

Do we really need a design system?

Yes,
we need.

But it's not about aesthetics.

It's about efficiency and consistency.
faster and cheaper deliveries.
focus on problems, not screens.
accessibility standards.
less code and shorter reviews.
smaller documentation.

and about money.

The Math

$ 14,000

is the cost for the company, with all taxes
included, to pay a designer average salary
in Rio de Janeiro, Brazil.
Average salary of a product designer, Glassdoor
estimate: $ 7,000

$ 80

is the amount paid for the hour worked,
considering that 1 month is equivalent to
176 work hours.

Being optimistic, we spend an average of
30 minutes a day redesigning, searching or
deciding which components to use.

$ 9,600

Spended in a year, considering:
2.5 hours per week.
10 hours per month.
12 months in a year.

$ 96,000

spended every
year for not having
a design system
with 10 designers in the team

or 10 iMacs

:)

The ouctomes

The presentation of these numbers to stakeholders was a success.

We were allowed to continue the work of building the design system, the devs would
be involved in the project and all the screens, from then on, would be made with
the new components.
